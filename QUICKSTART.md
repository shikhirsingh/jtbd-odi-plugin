# QUICKSTART — 2 minutes

You just installed the plugin. Here's the fastest possible path from zero to running.

---

## Step 1 — Type one of these, depending on you

```
/odihelp          ← if you're not sure what to do
/demo             ← if you want to see what the plugin produces before committing
/whatdoido        ← if you already have files/data and don't know what command to run
/preflight        ← if you want to know whether ODI is the right method for you at all
/brainstormjob    ← if you have an idea or are looking at a product and don't
                    even know what "the job" is yet — light web research helps you
                    figure out the underlying customer job
```

Any of those is a safe first move. All five are interactive — they ask questions before doing anything.

---

## Step 2 — That's it

The plugin guides you the rest of the way. Every command tells you what runs next.

Type `/demo`. The plugin will walk you through realistic outputs from a known case study (Bosch's CS20 circular saw — Ulwick's own example) without running anything real. ~5 minutes. No commitment.

## The four paths, in 30 seconds

| Path | What it is | Real survey? | Decision-grade? | Time | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `/runfullodi --mode real` | Full ODI — interviews + survey | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | 4–8 wks | $20k–$80k |
| `/researchpath` | Online research (no interviews) + REAL survey | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | 3–5 wks | $5k–$20k |
| `/runliteodi` | Qualitative only — interviews, no survey | ❌ No | 🟡 Directional only | 2–3 wks | $2k–$10k |
| `/run-synthetic-survey` | Synthetic respondents — no interviews, no real survey | ❌ No | ❌ Hypothesis only | 30–60 min | ~$0 |
